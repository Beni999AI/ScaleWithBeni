## 1. Brand Overview

**ScaleWithBeni** is a fully managed AI automation service for RERA-licensed real estate agents in Dubai. The brand represents one person — Beni — delivering enterprise-grade automation infrastructure with precision, directness, and a strong ROI orientation.

The brand is not a faceless SaaS product. It is a specialist operator who knows the Dubai real estate market, speaks the business language of agents, and takes full responsibility for the systems he builds. This personal accountability is the brand's core differentiating posture.

### Brand Positioning Statement

ScaleWithBeni is the only fully managed AI voice and messaging automation service built specifically for Dubai real estate agents — so every lead is answered, qualified, and followed up in their language, without the agent lifting a finger.

### One-Line Brand Description

AI automation for Dubai real estate.

## 2. Brand Personality

ScaleWithBeni's personality sits at the intersection of four traits:

| Trait | What It Looks Like | What It Never Looks Like |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct** | Short sentences, active verbs, no fluff | Passive voice, hedging language, academic tone |
| **Precise** | Specific numbers (3 rings, 60 seconds, 30 seconds) | Vague superlatives ("fast", "powerful", "cutting-edge") |
| **Confident** | Makes claims and backs them with logic | Overconfident hype, buzzwords, hollow promises |
| **Human** | Written by and about Beni as a real person | Corporate "we", faceless brand voice, generic agency speak |

The brand does not shout. It states facts, frames them in terms of deal value, and lets the math do the convincing.

## 3. Brand Voice and Tone of Voice

### Core Principle: ROI First, Features Never

Every piece of copy — email subject lines, website headlines, pricing descriptions, WhatsApp messages — must be anchored in deal value recovered, not in technology features. The audience is agents who think in AED commissions, not in API calls.

**Correct:** "One closed deal pays for 20+ months."
**Incorrect:** "Powered by GPT-4o and Vapi's advanced voice synthesis."

### Writing Rules

1. **Use specific numbers.** Replace "fast" with "under 3 rings." Replace "affordable" with "$299/month." Replace "saves time" with "saves 30 minutes per client match."
2. **Lead with the problem, then the solution.** Agents understand their own pain. Name it first.
3. **Never use the word "AI" as a selling point alone.** AI is a means to an end. The end is: no missed calls, no cold leads, no manual follow-up.
4. **Short sentences are always preferred.** If a sentence has two ideas, split it into two sentences.
5. **Avoid filler openers.** Never begin a message with "I hope this finds you well," "As you may know," or "At ScaleWithBeni, we believe."
6. **Use "you" and "your."** Address the agent directly in the second person. Do not use the royal "we" to create distance.
7. **Beni signs off as Beni.** Not "The ScaleWithBeni Team." Not "Support." Just Beni.

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Website headlines | Bold, urgent, declarative | "Your leads won't wait." |
| Website body copy | Clear, practical, ROI-anchored | "When a lead calls your number and you can't answer, our AI picks up within 3 rings, qualifies them, and books a meeting directly into your calendar. No missed calls. No lost deals." |
| Sales emails (cold) | Conversational, specific, brief | "Dubai leads expect a callback in under 2 minutes. Most agents call back in 4+ hours." |
| WhatsApp (client ops) | Casual but professional, async-friendly | "Done — your inbound fallback is live. If a direct caller doesn't get you, the AI picks up. I'll send you a test call log tomorrow." |
| FAQ / support copy | Plain, factual, reassuring | "None. ScaleWithBeni is a fully managed service. We build, test, monitor, and maintain everything." |
| Legal / terms | Neutral, precise, no softening | Standard legal language; do not inject brand personality into legal documents. |

### Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Cutting-edge | Specific technology name (e.g., GPT-4o, Vapi) |
| Seamless | Specific outcome (e.g., "books directly into your calendar") |
| Powerful / Robust | Specific stat (e.g., "answers in under 3 rings") |
| Revolutionary | (omit entirely) |
| Leverage | Use |
| Utilize | Use |
| Solutions | Systems / automation / service |
| Synergy | (never) |
| AI-powered (as a headline claim) | What the AI actually does |

## 4. Messaging Framework

### Primary Headline Family

The website headline establishes the brand's primary message structure — a problem statement followed by a rotating consequence that increases urgency:

```
"Your leads won't wait."
  → "Pick up within 3 rings."
  → "Even while you're mid-showing."
  → "Even at 3 AM."
  → "Qualify, book, and notify you."
```

This structure (problem → AI action) is the canonical ScaleWithBeni message format. Reuse it in ads, outreach, and pitches.

### Key Proof Points (Always Available)

These are factual, specific, and must be used consistently — never paraphrased loosely:

| Claim | Exact Wording | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | "AI picks up within 3 rings" | Service specification |
| Qualification speed | "Qualifies in under 30 seconds" | Service specification |
| Response time (outbound) | "Calls leads in under 60 seconds from form submission" | Service specification |
| ROI anchor | "One closed deal pays for 20+ months" | Financial model (Growth tier at $449/mo vs. AED 10K–30K commission) |
| Availability | "24/7 — no sick days, no time zones" | Service characteristic |
| Language reach | "Arabic, English, Russian, Hindi, and Mandarin" | Service specification |
| Call coverage | "100% of calls caught" | Service characteristic |

### Value Proposition Hierarchy

When communicating about ScaleWithBeni, messages should follow this priority order:

1. **No missed direct calls** — inbound fallback (highest intent leads never go to voicemail)
2. **No cold leads** — 60-second outbound response from portal form submission
3. **No manual follow-up** — automated WhatsApp and email sequences in the lead's language
4. **No tech burden** — fully managed; the agent does nothing technical

### Sales Anchoring Principle

From [./financial.md](./financial.md): Always anchor pricing to commission value recovered, not to cost comparison. Use this framing in every proposal and pricing conversation:

> "A single closed Dubai deal generates AED 10,000–30,000 in commission ($2,720–$8,170 USD). The Growth plan at $449/month costs $5,388 for a full year — less than one deal. The system pays for itself with one extra closed deal."

Never pitch on feature comparison to competitors. Never use "cheaper than hiring a VA" as a primary frame.

## 5. Visual Identity

### Color Palette

The site is **dark mode only**. There is no light mode version of ScaleWithBeni. The `<html>` element carries `class="dark"` permanently. Do not create light-mode variants of any branded material.

| Role | Name | Hex / Value | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Background | Near-black | `#050505` | Page background; never use pure black |
| Surface / Card | Dark navy | `#0f1629` | Card backgrounds, pricing tiles, modals |
| Secondary surface | Deep navy | `#1a1a2e` | Muted containers, secondary sections |
| Primary text | Soft white | `#eef2f7` | All body text, headlines |
| Muted text | Steel blue-grey | `#7a8ba8` | Secondary text, descriptions, captions |
| Primary accent ("Cyan") | Silver | `#ABABAB` | Primary buttons, active states, check marks, highlighted text, borders on featured elements |
| Accent dim | Silver/transparent | `rgba(171, 171, 171, 0.15)` | Subtle borders, dividers, glass-effect backgrounds |
| Ambient glow | Electric cyan | `rgba(0, 229, 255, 0.12)` | Background radial gradients only — CTA section atmospheric effect. Never use as a solid color. |
| Gold | Amber gold | `#e8a838` | Add-on pricing indicators, secondary emphasis |

**Color usage notes:**

- The primary accent color is referred to as "cyan" in the codebase (`--color-cyan`, `bg-cyan`, `text-cyan`) but its actual value is `#ABABAB` — a light silver-grey. This is intentional: the palette prioritises muted, premium sophistication over bright neon. Do not replace this with a true cyan blue.
- The electric cyan (`#00E5FF` / `rgba(0,229,255,...)`) appears only as an atmospheric glow — never as a text color or solid UI element.
- Gold (`#e8a838`) is a supporting accent for pricing add-ons. It is not a brand primary color and should not be used for general emphasis.
- Never use white (`#FFFFFF`) as a background or dominant surface. Off-white `#eef2f7` is the correct foreground text color.

### Typography

**Primary typeface:** Inter (Google Fonts)

Inter is the sole typeface for all ScaleWithBeni digital materials. No secondary typeface is used.

| Element | Size | Weight | Tracking | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hero headline (H1) | 4xl–7xl (responsive) | Regular (400) | `tracking-tighter` | The hero H1 uses font-regular, not bold — the animated line uses font-semibold |
| Section headlines (H2) | 3xl–5xl (responsive) | Bold (700) | `tracking-tighter` | All section headings use bold + tighter tracking |
| Card headings (H3) | lg | Semibold (600) | Default | |
| Body / description | base–xl (responsive) | Regular (400) | `tracking-tight` | `leading-relaxed` for readability |
| Navigation links | sm | Regular (400) | Default | Muted color; white on hover |
| Badges / labels | sm | Semibold (600) | Default | Used in pricing and service cards |
| Small print | sm | Regular (400) | Default | Footnotes, copyright, legal links |
| Buttons | sm–lg (by context) | Semibold (600) | Default | Primary CTA always semibold |

**Typography rules:**
- Never mix typefaces. Inter is the only font.
- Headlines always use `tracking-tighter`. Body always uses `tracking-tight` or default.
- Antialiasing (`font-smooth: antialiased`) is applied globally.
- Text is never centered in long paragraphs — only in short headlines and stat blocks.

### Spacing and Layout

- **Container:** `max-width` constrained, horizontally padded (`px-6`), centered with `mx-auto`
- **Section padding:** `py-20 md:py-28` — consistent across all content sections
- **Border radius:** `0.625rem` base (`--radius`); cards use `rounded-xl`
- **Grid system:** 1 column mobile, 2 columns tablet, 4 columns desktop for pricing; 3 columns for services
- **Borders:** Subtle, using `border-cyan-dim` (`rgba(171,171,171,0.15)`) — never harsh or high-contrast

### Motion and Animation

Animations are handled by `motion/react` (not `framer-motion`). Scroll-triggered animations use the `useIntersection` hook, which fires once at 20% viewport visibility — they do not repeat on scroll-back.

**Animation conventions:**

| Element | Animation | Parameters |
|---|---|---|
| Section content (on scroll) | Fade in + slide up | `opacity: 0→1`, `y: 20→0`, `duration: 0.5s` |
| Pricing cards (staggered) | Fade in + slide up | `delay: index × 0.1s` |
| Hero rotating text | Spring slide vertical | `type: "spring"`, `stiffness: 50` |
| Hero background beams | Continuous upward drift | Canvas-rendered, multi-layer parallax, subtle pulse |
| Hero film grain | Random noise overlay | Canvas-rendered, `opacity: 12/255` — almost invisible |

**Animation principles:**
- Animations are always subtle. They enhance without distracting.
- No animations loop or repeat after completion (except the hero rotating text and beam effect).
- Spring physics are used for text transitions; linear easing for layout reveals.
- Duration is always 0.5 seconds or less for scroll-triggered reveals.

## 7. UI Component Standards

### Buttons

Two button variants are used throughout the site:

**Primary CTA Button**
- Background: `bg-cyan` (`#ABABAB`)
- Text: `text-black`, `font-semibold`
- Hover: `hover:bg-cyan/90`
- Used for: "Book a Demo," "Get Started," "Start Growing," "Go Pro"
- Size: `lg` for hero and section CTAs; `sm` for navbar

**Secondary / Outline Button**
- Background: `bg-white/5` or transparent
- Border: `border-cyan-dim` (`rgba(171,171,171,0.15)`)
- Text: `text-white`
- Hover: `hover:bg-white/5` or `hover:bg-white/10`
- Used for: "See Pricing," "WhatsApp Us," non-highlighted tier CTAs

**Never use:**
- Colored buttons outside of these two variants
- Rounded-full pill buttons (all buttons use `--radius` = `0.625rem` base)

### Cards

- Background: `bg-card` (`#0f1629`)
- Border: `border-cyan-dim` by default; `border-cyan` (`#ABABAB`) for highlighted/featured state
- Border radius: `rounded-xl`
- Featured state (e.g., "Most Popular" pricing tier): `shadow-[0_0_40px_rgba(0,229,255,0.12)]` + `scale-[1.02]`

### Badges

- Background: `bg-cyan` (`#ABABAB`)
- Text: `text-black`, `font-semibold`
- No border
- Used for tier badges ("Most Popular") and service badges ("Growth+", "Pro+")

### Navigation

- Fixed position, `z-50`
- Transparent when at top of page
- On scroll: `bg-background/80 backdrop-blur-lg border-b border-cyan-dim`
- Nav link color: `text-muted-foreground` at rest; `text-white` on hover

### Separators and Dividers

- Color: `bg-cyan-dim` (`rgba(171,171,171,0.15)`)
- Used as `border-t border-cyan-dim` on sections and as `<Separator>` components in the footer

## 8. Section Structure and Content Rules

The homepage sections appear in this fixed order:

```
Navbar
  → PremiumHero
  → SocialProof (stats bar)
  → Services
  → HowItWorks
  → Pricing
  → FAQ
  → CTA
Footer
```

**Rules for each section type:**

**Headlines:** Every section headline uses the format of one plain statement followed optionally by a colored (`text-cyan`) second phrase. Example: `"Simple Pricing."` + `"Serious ROI."`

**Stats bar (SocialProof):** Four stats only — Answer Time, Always On, Deal ROI, Call Coverage. Do not add stats without updating the `STATS` array in `constants.ts` and validating the claim against service specifications.

**Services:** Three services only. Badges indicate minimum required tier (`"Growth+"`, `"Pro+"`). Service order: Inbound Call → Follow-Up → Property Matching.

**Pricing:** Four tiers in order: Starter → Growth → Pro → Team. Growth is always the visually highlighted tier (Most Popular). Do not reorder tiers.

**FAQ:** Ten questions covering speed, languages, technical requirements, inbound fallback, AI disclosure, cost, cancellation, portals, setup time, and company location. Maintain this coverage — do not remove legal or compliance-adjacent questions.

**CTA section:** Radial gradient glow background (`rgba(0,229,255,0.12)`). Two buttons: primary "Book a Demo" (cal.com link) and secondary "WhatsApp Us." No other CTAs.

## 10. What ScaleWithBeni Is Not

These positioning directions are explicitly off-brand. Avoid them in all copy and design decisions.

| Off-Brand Direction | Why It's Wrong |
|---|---|
| "AI for everyone" / generic automation | ScaleWithBeni is for one market: Dubai real estate agents. Specificity is the brand. |
| Feature-led messaging ("meet our 7 AI modules") | The audience doesn't care about features. They care about deals closed and calls not missed. |
| Startup hype ("disrupting real estate") | The brand is precise and grounded, not evangelical. |
| Enterprise visual identity (blues, corporate whites, serif fonts) | Dark, premium, personal — not corporate. |
| Friendly/casual chatbot aesthetic | This is serious business infrastructure, not a consumer app. |
| "We" as a large team ("our team of experts") | ScaleWithBeni is Beni. One person. Acknowledge it — it's a strength, not a weakness. |
| Light mode design | Dark mode only. Always. The visual identity depends on the dark background. |

## 11. Arabic Language Brand Considerations

Given that Arabic is a primary language of service delivery (Gulf dialect and Modern Standard Arabic), the following rules apply to all Arabic-language brand touchpoints:

- **Arabic scripts in marketing materials must be reviewed by a native Gulf Arabic speaker.** Do not publish AI-generated Arabic copy without native review.
- Arabic text is always right-to-left (RTL). Ensure any digital template that includes Arabic supports RTL rendering.
- The brand name "ScaleWithBeni" is not translated — it is used in Latin script in all languages. No Arabic transliteration of the brand name is needed.
- When including Arabic in mixed-language contexts (e.g., a multilingual tagline), set Arabic text in a font that properly renders Arabic glyphs (Inter supports Arabic via its Arabic subset; confirm this is loaded).
- The Arabic PS used in multilingual WhatsApp messages: `"نتحدث العربية أيضاً — أخبرنا بلغتك المفضلة"` — this is approved copy. Do not rephrase without native review.

## 12. Brand Don'ts Summary

| Don't | Do Instead |
|---|---|
| Use the brand in light mode | Dark mode only |
| Use true cyan (`#00E5FF`) as a solid UI color | Use it only as an ambient glow gradient |
| Use any font other than Inter | Inter only |
| Sign messages as "The ScaleWithBeni Team" | Sign as "Beni" |
| Lead with AI technology in copy | Lead with the business outcome |
| Use vague claims ("fast", "smart", "seamless") | Use specific numbers ("under 3 rings", "60 seconds", "30 seconds") |
| Place the logo on light backgrounds | Dark backgrounds only |
| Add a light/dark toggle to the website | Dark mode is hardcoded; it's a brand decision |
| Publish Arabic copy without native review | Native review required before any Arabic template goes live |
| Pitch on feature comparison | Pitch on ROI and deal value recovered |
